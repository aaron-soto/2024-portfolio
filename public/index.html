I was born and raised in Casa Grande, Arizona. After graduating high school, I joined the military and became an Army Ranger, serving as a sergeant in the 75th Ranger Regiment. This role allowed me to travel the world, participate in the D-Day reenactment in Normandy, France, and earn my HALO (High Altitude Low Opening) military skydiving license, which led to my civilian skydiving license.

Upon leaving the military, I worked in Arizona building RVs and Toy Haulers. While I enjoyed this job, I aspired to do something bigger. I began teaching myself programming and, once confident in my skills, enrolled in the Coding Dojo bootcamp to further my education in software development.

After completing the bootcamp, I quickly secured a position with a consulting company, working on various projects from simple Node.js servers to large-scale applications utilizing AWS and multiple repositories. During this time, I also started my own side business, AyeZeeWebDesigns, creating websites for local businesses.

In my free time, I love building things, whether it's crafting wood furniture, using my 3D printer, or operating my CNC machine. I'm always working on something new. Additionally, I am constantly learning new technologies to incorporate into my projects.
